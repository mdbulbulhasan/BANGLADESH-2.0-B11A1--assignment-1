Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

The pillars of Bangladesh 2.0 innovation are Smart Citizen, Smart Government, Smart Society, and Smart Economy. These pillars are part of the Smart Bangladesh.

Sustainability

The pillars of sustainability in Bangladesh 2.0 include economic diversification, infrastructure development, and social reforms.

Infrastructure

Building Bangladesh 2.0 requires a significant investment in infrastructure, including transportation, healthcare, and information communication technology.

Empowerment

The Bangladesh 2.0 vision also strongly emphasises social reforms. Despite the progress made in reducing poverty and improving social indicators.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, is renowned for pioneering microfinance and founding Grameen Bank, who has been serving as Chief Adviser of the Interim Government of Bangladesh since 8 August 2024. Born in 1940, he developed an innovative model to provide small, collateral-free loans to the impoverished, particularly women, enabling them to start businesses and break the cycle of poverty. His vision of empowering individuals through financial inclusion has transformed millions of lives and inspired similar initiatives worldwide. Beyond microfinance, Yunus advocates for social business—entrepreneurship aimed at solving social problems. His groundbreaking efforts have earned global recognition, establishing him as a beacon of hope for sustainable development and poverty eradication."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus
